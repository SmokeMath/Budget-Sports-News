Introduction: A Look Into the WNBA

The Women's National Basketball Association was founded on April 22, 1996 and is headquartered in New York. Currently, there are 12 teams, with a number of players being international and coming from Russia, China, Poland, Australia, Portugal, Canada, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines (Caribbean), and Brazil.

Fun Facts:

  • The WNBA ball size is 28.5" in circumference, 1" smaller than the NBA ball size.
  • Beginning in 2020, players are granted 100% of their base salary during pregnancy.
  • The average length of time in the WNBA is 5 years.
  • 2/3 of the WNBA head coaches are women.
  • Only 3 teams exist from the very first WNBA season.
  • Magic Johnson co-owns the Los Angeles Sparks.
  • Sue Bird is (unfortunately) not related to Larry Bird.
